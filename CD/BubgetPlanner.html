The Principles

Quiet rules
for a confident
financial life.

i.

Pay yourself first

Automate investments on the day your salary arrives. Saving must not be a residual decision — it should be the first one.

ii.

Insurance ≠ investment

Term insurance for life cover. Health insurance for medical risk. Mutual funds for wealth. Never let a single product try to do all three.

iii.

Diversify with intention

Equity for growth. Debt for stability. Gold for insurance. The exact ratio depends on your age, your appetite, and your horizon — not on tips.

iv.

Beware lifestyle inflation

When income rises, increase your savings rate before your spending. The most dangerous wealth destroyer is the one you never notice.
